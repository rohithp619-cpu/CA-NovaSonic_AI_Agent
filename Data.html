Even the optimistic case clears barely 7% of program cost on ad revenue alone — direct monetization isn't the justification for this program.

Why this still pays back: the case isn't ad-revenue uplift — it's retention. Lifting the 12-month active rate in this tier by 10 points (55% → 65%) keeps roughly 204 creators who'd otherwise have to be replaced. At an estimated $2,500 in acquisition and ramp cost per replacement creator, that's ~$510,000 in avoided cost — over 95% of the program's first-year value. Treat the ad-revenue line as a small bonus, not the thesis, when this goes to budget.
