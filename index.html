NovaSonic has scaled to 500,000 listeners and 12,000 creators, but the creator side of the business is leaking: only 22% of creators are still active at six months, and listening attention is heavily concentrated in a small slice of the catalogue. Viewed through a growth & acquisition lens, creators aren't just supply — they're an acquisition channel, and a starved one limits how far the platform can grow.

Three reinforcing challenges, identified against the case's real anchors and an illustrative supporting dataset.

CHALLENGE 1

The activation cliff is decided in 60 days, not 6 months

21% of creators reach 100 cumulative listens by day 60 — almost exactly matching the 22% still active at 6 months. Survival is decided early, not gradually.

CHALLENGE 2

The discoverable catalogue is ~2,400 creators, not 12,000

The bottom 80% of creators (9,600 people) collectively earn just 5% of all listens — functionally invisible to new listeners.

CHALLENGE 3

Discovery amplifies the already-big and ignores everyone else

The top 3% get 65% of their listens from on-platform discovery; everyone else gets 18%, doing 82% of their own audience-building externally.

A three-agent pipeline — Researcher, Manager, Communicator — was run against Challenge 1. The Researcher diagnosed root causes using real external research plus NovaSonic's own data; the Manager converted the highest-leverage cause into a 90-day operational plan; the Communicator wrote the actual creator-facing campaign the plan calls for. Each handoff is quoted verbatim by the receiving agent, so the chain is auditable end to end.

Risk classification

This workflow is an AI-assisted research and analysis exercise: a consultant feeds Claude synthetic, aggregate platform metrics (listener counts, retention cohorts, listen concentration by tier) and gets back diagnostic findings and a growth business case. Claude does not decide anything about any individual creator or user; it produces a document that human leadership reviews and acts on (or doesn't). That places it at minimal risk under the EU AI Act's tiering, not high-risk: it is not listed in Annex III (no employment decisions, no credit or essential-services eligibility scoring, no biometric or law-enforcement use), and there is a human in the loop between the AI's output and any real-world action. The flag matters though: you confirmed in Q4 that decisions stemming from this report can change how users interact with the service (discovery exposure, monetization eligibility). If NovaSonic later automates any of those changes (an algorithmic discovery gate, an automatic monetization cutoff) without human review, that downstream system would need its own classification, and given it touches people's income and visibility on the platform, it could land much closer to high-risk. A fundamental rights impact assessment is not required for this consultation workflow as described, but would likely be warranted for any automated implementation of its recommendations.

Five-point hygiene check

Bias FAIL

The growth model in the source document explicitly funds only the "next 17%" tier and leaves the bottom 50% (6,000 creators, 1% of listens) out of any intervention, which risks the analysis itself hardening the popularity concentration it identifies as a problem.

Privacy PASS

The input data is explicitly labelled synthetic/illustrative aggregate metrics, not real personal, customer, or employee records.

Consent FAIL

The document itself says to "replace with NovaSonic's real ARPU, CAC, and listen data before using this for a budget decision," and no consent or lawful-basis process exists yet for when real, individual-level creator data replaces those placeholders.

Attribution PASS

The source document clearly marks which figures are synthetic versus real, and instructs the user not to use placeholder figures for actual budget decisions.

Transparency PASS

The stated audience (NovaSonic's leadership and strategy team) explicitly knows AI was used to produce the analysis.

Remediation

  1. Before this report (or any version of it) informs a real budget or policy decision, swap in NovaSonic's actual metrics and put a documented lawful basis and consent pathway in place for the real, creator-level data behind those metrics; right now that pathway does not exist, only synthetic placeholders do.
  2. Re-scope the growth program so the bottom 50% long-tail tier is explicitly assessed rather than silently excluded; funding only the established tier, while leaving the long-tail with zero intervention, should be a stated and justified choice, not a default.
Note: This is general education on the EU AI Act, not legal advice.

H4 — Onboarding/product friction (low). Standard "rule out the boring explanation" hypothesis.

H5 — Non-top creators lack a pre-existing external audience (medium, as a refinement of H1). "Bring your own audience" only works if you already have one.

Recommended 2-week validation sprint: pull impression logs for activated vs. non-activated cohorts, control for content-quality confounds, check for a literal cold-start eligibility gate, then go/no-go on a discovery-surface intervention.
